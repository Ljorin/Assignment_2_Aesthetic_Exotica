What are Cryptids?

Cryptids are creatures claimed to be existing somewhere in the wild but aren’t scientifically proven.

Cryptids and the urban legends that birth them are found all over the world. Scotland has the Loch Ness Monster, North America has Sasquatch, South America has Chupacabra. Of course there are much more out there and listing them all would be a pain.

In the slideshow below, dear reader, you will learn about creatures and their lore, famous and obscure, with brief descriptions. This list by no means covers all of the creatures nor is it fully in-depth but it’ll serve as a basis for you to start your own research.

Ghosts

Apparitions of those who passed, either manifesting fully opaque, hauntingly translucent, or completely invisible to the naked eye save for their presence. Some spirits are harmless while others are more aggressive and dangerous.

Sometimes, a spirit may be deceiving you, claiming to be a ghost when it may be something more malevolent. Good rule of thumb, don’t engage with spirits unless you are sure.

Learn more here.

Aliens

E.T.s or extraterrestrials, are lifeforms originating not from Earth. Depending on which planet an alien comes from, one might call Mars’ Martians, Venus’ Venutians, Jupiter’s Jovians, and so on. Some believe these lifeforms live among us and are manipulating our society.

There are several types of aliens believed to be existing somewhere among the stars. These include: the Greys, the Nordic Aliens, and the Little Green Men.

Learn more here.

Vampires

Fanged creatures with an aversion to sunlight, vampires are powerful undead who stalk the night for the taste and sustenance of blood. These blood leeches can be deterred by garlic-- good reason to get garlic bread.

The most famous vampires you might think of are Count Dracula of Bram Stoker’s Dracula and Edward Cullen from the Twilight series which enthralled many impressionable teenage girls about a sparkling, pale dude.
